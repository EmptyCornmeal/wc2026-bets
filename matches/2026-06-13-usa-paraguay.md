## Learnings
- **The two analyst-flagged problems both bit, and the bet died on them — not on
  football.** (1) Placed at 3.90 below the 4.00 floor (#min-odds-discipline); (2)
  the optional Under 3.5 leg failed the risk test — it directly contradicted the
  match thesis (a USA siege that scores "decided by one Pulisic/Balogun moment"
  wants goals, not an unders cap). Stacking unders on a favourite-siege card is the
  same family of error as #leg-redundancy. The 3-leg builder (USA + Paraguay cards
  + Pulisic shots) was also dead, but on the prop, not the total. #optional-legs
- **Predicted weakest leg was wrong AGAIN, and informatively.** Called USA-to-win
  as the risk; USA won 4-1 and the props/totals legs killed it. Three settlements
  in, the "result" leg has been the killer zero times — the leg-construction risk
  is consistently in the goalscorer/shots/totals legs, not the match odds.
  #killer-is-never-the-result
- **Pulisic = creator, not shooter, and the prop ignored role.** We even wrote
  "Pulisic on most shots" in the pre-match read, but his actual game is assists/
  crosses; a "2+ shots" line on a creator who gets rested with a big lead is a poor
  leg. "To score or assist" or "1+ shot" would have been the role-appropriate pick.
  Pairs with the Canada #striker-roulette lesson: price the player's actual job.
  #prop-role-fit
- Super Sub fired (Pulisic → Berhalter) but a defensive sub can't rescue an
  attacking prop — SS only helps when the replacement plays the same role. #super-sub
- Insurance note: two losing legs, so Bet Builder Insurance (one-leg-loss refund)
  would not have triggered here either.
