## Learnings
- **Predicted weakest leg (Jonathan David anytime) did lose — call confirmed.** But
  it was *not* the sole killer: Match Odds also went down with the late equaliser,
  so the slip would have lost on the draw alone. Two attacking-dependent legs, both
  failed; the two suppression legs (corners, unders) both won. #weakest-leg-confirmed
- **The suppression read was excellent, the result read was wrong.** As at Mexico,
  the edge was in the control/suppression legs (corners + unders landed comfortably),
  while the two-way and prop legs that need a *specific* attacking outcome (Canada win,
  David goal) are where slips die. Lean the build toward suppression, not on the
  favourite converting. #suppression-legs
- **Super Sub red herring, again (#super-sub):** the slip showed Jonathan David ⇄
  Promise David, and Promise David *did* come on and set up the equaliser — but the
  leg was "anytime goalscorer" and neither David scored, so the swap was cosmetic to
  the result. Verify the actual scoring event, not the badge.
- Transferable rule: on a favourite-control slip, the corners/unders legs are the
  reliable money; do not let an anytime-scorer + match-odds pairing (two legs that
  both require the favourite to actually win and score) dominate the price.
  #favourite-control-trap
